Pre-Launch Summary

Hypothesis validated by data — 40x drop between gaming (~90%) and partner (~2%) completion suggests comprehension friction. Language is a plausible barrier in non-English geos.

1. Experiment Design

Background

Problem: Tradeit mission copy is English-only in non-English geos (LATAM, MENA, Turkey). Partner challenge completion is ~2% while gaming completion is ~90%. Users complete the gaming task (play 1 match) but skip partner actions (trade, link account). Precise comprehension matters for partner tasks (“Link your account”, “Trade any item”, “Trade $250+”) — language friction may explain the 40x drop-off.

Solution: Localise mission titles, challenge descriptions, CTAs, and reward labels into users’ local language. Keep reward values, mission structure, and UX unchanged. Measure the impact on partner challenge completion.

Why Geo Before/After: Tradeit campaigns are configured per geo with specific copy. User-level A/B would require engineering for within-geo bucketing. Geo-level before/after ships immediately with zero engineering: compare EN baseline (prior month) to localised copy (treatment month), using non-test EN geos as seasonality control.

7. Decision Framework

Key Questions (to answer post-experiment)

Does localisation lift partner completion?

Primary question. Comparing partner challenge completion in localised geos vs their own EN baseline. Must see ≥15% relative lift to declare success.

Which challenge step benefits most?

Challenge-to-challenge progression will reveal whether localisation helps at account linkage, first trade, or deeper trade tiers. This determines whether to localise all copy or focus on specific CTAs.

Does the partner landing page gap limit the effect?

Users click a localised CTA but land on tradeit.gg in English. If we see lift on early steps (AL) but not later (trade), the EN landing page may be the bottleneck. This informs Phase 2 scope.

Is the effect consistent across geos?

LATAM (Portuguese) and Turkey (Turkish) may respond differently. If one geo lifts and the other doesn’t, the insight is geo-specific rather than universal.

Next Steps (Immediate)

  1. Run geo-sizing query — Confirm LATAM and Turkey have ≥37K monthly Tradeit users.
  2. Brief translation vendor — Send copy list (titles, descriptions, CTAs, reward labels) with gaming/esports context.
  3. Pull baseline data — Per-geo partner completion rates for the prior EN month.
  4. Align with Activation Ops — Confirm campaign setup timeline and monitoring plan.
  5. Notify Tradeit (Commercial) — Informational only, no partner action.
