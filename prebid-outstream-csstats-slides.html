GA page views balanced: Moba 4,728 US / Primis 4,542 US — the gap is supply-side, not traffic-side.

Why this matters

  • Primis has a supply-side fill problem in the US — 499 impressions from 4.5K page views is near-zero fill
  • Moba's US revenue ($125.22) is 68% of its total — the single biggest driver of the +90% gap
  • This is not a traffic split issue — GA page views are balanced at 51.0% / 49.0%

Could a hybrid work?

Moba US + Primis RoW saves ~$39/month in ex-US markets where Primis beats Moba ($97.61 vs ~$59). Not worth the operational complexity for $39/month.

Methodology

Experiment Setup & Data Sources

Setup

  • Platform: CSStats.gg — outstream video placement
  • A/B split: 50/50 via GA experiment (ads_ab_variant)
  • Duration: 25 days (30 Mar – 23 Apr 2026)
  • Page views: Moba 4.88M / Primis 4.85M
  • Traffic split: 50.15% / 49.85% (Δ 0.6% — valid)

Data Sources

  • GA4 page views by experiment cohort & geo
  • GAM daily × country report for Moba (1,835 rows)
  • Primis aggregated country report (58 geos)
  • Both Primis and Moba revenues confirmed as net — no additional fees

Key Decisions

  • RPV as primary metric — Revenue per Page View normalises for the balanced traffic split
  • Net comparison — Both Primis and Moba report net revenue. Direct comparison, no fees to deduct
  • Impression count not comparable — Primis serves 5.3x more impressions at dramatically lower eCPM. Volume alone does not indicate value
  • US fill investigated — Primis supply-side gap (499 imp) confirmed as fill issue, not traffic issue. GA page views balanced
