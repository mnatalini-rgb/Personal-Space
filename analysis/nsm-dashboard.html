6. Key Insights

1. C2 Dominates Value

The C2 conversion (Trade $250+) is the primary driver of value, accounting for 61-66% of the total € generated across both months.

2. CIS Volume vs Efficiency

CIS represents 79% of the total EBU audience, but suffers from a low AL rate (1.6-2.3% compared to NA's 4-5%), keeping its efficiency down.

3. Shifting Regional Momentum

MoM efficiency diverged significantly: NA's €/1K EBU declined by -18.4%, whereas CIS saw substantial growth of +24.0%.

4. BOFU is the C2 Engine

The BOFU segment yields the highest €/1K EBU by far, operating as a pure C2 generation engine, though constrained by a small audience size.

5. Untracked Opportunities

High-value actions like Trade $1,000+ (€35.00) and frequent actions like Trade 5 (€0.18) remain untracked in current missions, representing untapped potential.

Reconciling the Two Metrics

Removing the modal may get slightly more users to start linking (+12.8% full-pop AL rate), but it dramatically reduces completion among those who do (−48.4% at the linkage step). Since partner value is driven by completed linkages that convert to C1/C2, the linkage-step metric is the one that matters for NSM: +€416/cohort, +€9.82 per 1K users.

Recommendation: Keep the Trust Modal

Removing the trust modal cuts linkage-step completion nearly in half (5.34% → 2.75%, z=17.98, p < 0.001). While the full-population variant shows +12.8% raw AL rate, this marginal volume gain (~462 incremental ALs over 2M users) is far outweighed by the 48% quality loss at the conversion step. The trust modal generates €416 more value per cohort and €9.82 more per 1K users. The modal should remain as the permanent default for all partner AL flows. 9. Mission Lifecycle Performance

Most mission completions cluster in weeks 1–2 of a 4+ week campaign. Weeks 3–4 are largely dead weight — the mission is live but not driving incremental conversions. This front-loaded decay directly impacts NSM efficiency.

The Problem
  • Completions drop sharply after week 2
  • €/1K EBU craters in the campaign's back half
  • EBU stays inflated (activated but dormant users still counted)
  • Mission slot occupied with no incremental value
What to Measure
  • Completion curve — completions per week within campaign lifecycle (week 1, 2, 3, 4)
  • Active participants — users who completed ≥1 challenge that week (vs total EBU)
  • Engagement half-life — week by which 50% of total completions have occurred
  • Weekly €/1K active EBU — NSM efficiency using active (not nominal) EBU
Levers & Actions
  • Shorter campaigns (2-week sprints vs 4-week marathons)
  • Mid-campaign reward refreshes or challenge rotations
  • Re-engagement nudges targeting week 3–4 dormant users
  • Back-half experiments (higher rewards, mystery box, etc.)
Data source: weekly_mission_completions.json already captures completions per partner × challenge × week. Cross-reference with campaign start/end dates from CampaignService.Campaigns to derive lifecycle week (1, 2, 3, 4).
Implementation: Add lifecycle chart to each partner's Missions tab + surface engagement half-life as a KPI.
