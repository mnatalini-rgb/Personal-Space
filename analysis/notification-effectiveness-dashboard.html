Activity Notification Effectiveness

Mission Campaign Notifications · Completion-Focused Analysis · Updated 27 Apr 2026

Moritz Natalini · Ads & Partnerships · EFG

Primary metric: Challenge completion rate by notification cohort (Clicked / Impression / No Notification)

Verdict

Notification clicks are THE conversion engine for partner challenges — Deposit +11.39pp, Trade +10.24pp, Account Link +7.53pp completion uplift when users click vs no notification

EE notifications drive 2.6× higher full completion than Traditional — 7.91% vs 3.01% full completion rate when clicked. EE click uplift is +4.70pp vs Traditional's +1.21pp

Notification impact is 10× stronger for sponsored vs Premium missions — Premium users complete regardless (93-99% baseline); sponsored challenges are click-dependent (Trade 1.22% → 11.95% with click = 9.8×)

Win Match and Bet notifications are counterproductive — negative uplift across ALL partners (users who win/bet do so regardless)

Per-partner patterns diverge significantly — Winline Play Match +17.81pp vs WhiteMarket +4.97pp — same challenge type, different impact

1. Analysis Design

Background

Problem: Activity notifications are the primary touchpoint driving users back to missions after auto-join, task completion, and reward availability. We had no visibility into which notification types drive actual challenge completions vs just generating clicks.

Solution: Joined notification interaction data with mission completion data to measure completion rates across three cohorts: Clicked, Impression Only, and No Notification. Added Premium mission benchmarks and EE vs Traditional notification comparison.

Why: Clicks mean nothing if they don't convert to completions. This analysis reframes notification effectiveness around the metric that matters for partner value: challenge completion.

Notifications matter MORE for sponsored challenges

Premium missions show 90-97% completion without notifications — users complete gaming tasks naturally. Sponsored challenges (Deposit 3.65%, Trade 1.22%, Buy 0.17%) have near-zero baseline completion. The notification click is the only path to meaningful partner conversion rates.

Premium April Showers: +62pp click uplift

This is by far the largest click uplift in any campaign. The 36.69% no-notification baseline vs 98.79% clicked suggests April Showers has harder tasks than typical Premium missions (avg 2.94 tasks vs ~1 for older months). The notification is critical here.

Relative multiplier tells the real story

Trade completion: 1.22% → 11.95% = 9.8× with click. Deposit: 3.65% → 16.52% = 4.5×. For Premium tasks the multiplier is 1.1-2.7×. Notifications have disproportionate leverage on partner value challenges.

Season 8 Prestige Path: An outlier

3.49% clicked / 1.15% impression / 0.40% no-notif. This is a long-term progression path (not a mission) with extremely low completion across all cohorts. Notifications still show a 3.1× multiplier, but absolute rates are negligible.

EE clicks convert 2.6× better to full completion

When a user clicks an EE notification, they're 2.6× more likely to complete all campaign tasks vs clicking a standard lifecycle notification. This confirms EE's targeting of stuck users who need that final push to finish.

Traditional drives higher task volume

Traditional clicked users average 3.84 tasks vs EE's 1.25. This reflects different user journeys: Traditional covers the full lifecycle (auto-join, first task, mid-campaign) while EE targets late-stage re-engagement on fewer remaining tasks.

Data quality note

Any-completion rate shows 100% for EE due to a counting methodology artifact (users receiving multiple EE frameworks for the same campaign inflate the completion numerator). Full completion rate is unaffected and used as the primary comparison metric throughout.

9. Decision Framework

Key Questions

Which notifications should we suppress?

Win Match and Bet — negative completion uplift across all partners. Suppressing reduces fatigue without losing conversions.

Where should EE Framework expand next?

Trade (Tradeit) and Buy (WhiteMarket) — 100% click-dependent challenges where EE's 2.6× higher full completion rate directly multiplies partner value.

Is notification investment justified for Premium missions?

Premium missions show 90-97% completion without notifications. Notification ROI is dramatically higher for sponsored challenges where baseline is near-zero. Prioritise sponsored notification quality over Premium volume.

Next Steps

  1. Suppress Bet and Win Match notifications (no conversion cost, reduces fatigue)
  2. Expand EE Framework to Trade (Tradeit) and Buy (WhiteMarket) challenge types
  3. Investigate AL/Educational notification coverage gap for Tradeit and WhiteMarket
  4. Optimise Deposit notification copy/timing for Winline (16.55% clicked CR has room to grow)
  5. A/B test notification frequency cap to combat sawtooth decay
  6. Run Part B of EE vs Traditional query for per-framework completion breakdown ✓ Done — see Section 5b
  7. Prioritise Reward Motivation as default EE framework for all partners (12.19% full CR, 2× next best)
