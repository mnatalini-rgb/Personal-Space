### Product-Side Gap
- Lack of engaging rewards for free and premium players — limits mission completion rates and partner value

Revenue targets that depend on commercial deal closure are outside PM control. The NSM (TAVI) was designed partly to address this — it measures product-driven value, not deal-driven revenue.

## 3. What to Implement, Let Go, and Continue

### Implement (New Behaviours)

**Radical Focus via NSM**
- Use the TAVI framework to say "no" to requests that don't move OVU or AVU
- Every initiative should have a clear NSM connection before committing eng resources
- Default response to ad-hoc requests: "How does this move TAVI?"

**Communicate & Evangelise Wins**
- Post regularly to `#bet-ads-partnerships` (currently silent — needs 1-2 posts/week minimum)
- Update the weekly product review slide with numbers and outcomes, not just status
- Proactively share results with Commercial team — don't wait for them to ask
- Frame wins in terms stakeholders care about: partner value, revenue impact, user engagement
- Reference: `docs/templates/slack-channel-templates.md` and `docs/templates/weekly-slide-prep.md`

### Let Go

**High-Tension Interactions**
- Stop engaging in heated debates during meetings — especially with peers at similar level
- When disagreements arise, diffuse with data: "Let me pull the numbers and come back"
- Operate at Principal/Director communication level: calm, evidence-based, solution-oriented
- The goal is to be seen as the person who brings clarity, not the person who brings heat

### Continue

**Brand Partner Value / Outcomes Focus**
- Keep building products that drive measurable partner outcomes (account linkage, conversions)
- The conversion ladder approach (AL → C1 → C2 → C3) is working — double down
- Partner value storytelling (€3.1M Winline, €1M Tradeit) is a strength — keep quantifying

## 4. Skills to Enhance

### Executive Influence
- Build relationships with Director+ stakeholders beyond the immediate team
- Present data and recommendations in formats that executives consume (1-page summaries, not deep-dives)
- Practice the "so what" — every data point needs a recommendation attached
- Goal: be invited to strategic discussions, not just product reviews

### Vertical Expansion (Proactive, Not Reactive)
- Don't wait for Commercial to bring partnership opportunities — push proactively
- Identify verticals where FACEIT's audience data is valuable (gaming peripherals, energy drinks, betting, fintech)
- Build "pitch-ready" audience packages: segment size, engagement data, past campaign results
- Goal: generate inbound interest from new verticals through product capability demos

### Root-Cause Analysis
- When metrics move (up or down), go deeper than surface-level explanation
- Build a habit of asking "why" 3 times before concluding
- Document root-cause analyses — they become reusable knowledge for the team
- Example: the US revenue anomaly ($67.84 Moba vs $0.29 Publift) needs this treatment

### Partnership Experience Design
- Move beyond transactional integrations (show ad → track click) toward experiences that showcase synergy
- Example: Logitech Branded Event was a step in this direction — custom event driving Logitech KPIs
- Build a portfolio of "reference partnerships" that demonstrate the full value chain
- Goal: each major partner should have a case study showing product-driven outcomes
