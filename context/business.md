# Business Context

## Who we are

Product Manager at FACEIT.com, responsible for monetisation products.

## Product Areas

### 1. Brand Integrations
- **Products**: Missions, Branded Events, Drops
- **Model**: Performance-oriented — drives verified user actions for partners
- **Capabilities**: Account linkage, CDP integrated with advertiser CRM signals
- **Visibility**: Full customer journey across FACEIT side (activation, engagement, reward claims) and advertiser side (account creation, KYC, deposits, purchases, retention)

### 2. Advertising
- **Products**: Display + outstream video, programmatic/indirect, match takeover
- **Model**: Monetise user attention and inventory through measurable ad delivery
- **Key signals**: Viewability, CTR, video completion, fill rate, eCPM, inventory coverage
- **Scale**: ~2M MAU, heavily concentrated in Russia (CS2-dependent). ~20% of total monetisation revenues.
- **Current setup**: Publift for remnant inventory (~$50K/month), Mobalitics prebid in testing
- **Full context**: `context/advertising.md`

## Platform Context
- FACEIT is a competitive gaming platform
- Relevant surfaces: hubs, missions, competitions, matchmaking, anti-cheat
- Users are segmented by geo, platform, and engagement level

## Strategic Framing
- We optimise for **advertiser value created**, not internal revenue
- Revenue is a business outcome but a weak NSM — it is distorted by pricing, seasonality, geo mix, and partner constraints
- Our products serve two distinct advertiser needs: performance outcomes (BI) and qualified attention (Ads)

## Active Cross-Cutting Initiatives

Two strategic bets in early experimentation — monetisation model not yet confirmed for either. Full context: `context/initiatives.md`

- **Skin Vault** — expose user CS2 skin inventory on FACEIT profile via Steam API. Ready to experiment (top 300 profiles, 50/50 split). Primary monetisation hypothesis: Tradeit CPC.
- **Peripheral Initiative** — detect user hardware via FACEIT client (CPU, keyboard, mouse, device). Releasing at 1% Monday. Enables hardware-based ad targeting for peripheral brands (e.g. Logitech). Privacy review pending.
