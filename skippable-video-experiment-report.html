Experiment · A/B Test

Skippable Video Interstitial

Verdict

Retention guardrail passed — variant tracks within ±1K of control across 31 days (+0.3% delta, not statistically significant)

70.4% video completion rate — weighted avg across 184 countries (GAM); 83.3% full-15s completion on front-end events

0.37% CTR — 23,698 clicks to Premium purchase page across 6.47M impressions, driving 320 subscriptions

1. Experiment Design

Problem: Interstitial ads deliver highest CPMs due to strong visibility and CTR, but are highly intrusive. Increasing inventory volume risks user frustration and long-term CPM degradation. Revenue cannot safely grow by adding more interstitials.

Solution: Maintain same inventory size but increase CPMs by upgrading the creative format — replace static image interstitials with video. Video drives stronger advertiser outcomes (viewability, CTR, brand lift) at the same impression count.

Why skippable: Given the format's intrusiveness, skippable video (3s unskippable, then skip available) balances revenue uplift with user experience. Aligns with established user expectations (YouTube-like behaviour).

Test Groups

Control: Standard display interstitial

Key Design Choice

No Paid Cannibalisation: The video interstitial only fires when no display interstitial fills from the programmatic waterfall. Every video impression is incremental — it monetises previously unfilled inventory.

Guardrail Passed

Variant tracks within ±1K of control on all 31 days. +0.3% aggregate delta is not statistically significant — both groups show identical weekly seasonality (Sat/Sun peaks at ~190–197K, midweek troughs at ~151–165K). No retention divergence detected.

Weekly Pattern

Both groups exhibit strong 7-day cycle: matchmaking uniques peak on weekends (Sat 190K+) and trough midweek (Tue/Wed ~151–165K). Video interstitial does not dampen or shift natural engagement rhythm.
