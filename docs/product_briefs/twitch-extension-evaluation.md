**Author:** Moritz Natalini

## Background

Fahad (Commercial) proposed building a Twitch extension for IEM Cologne Major Pick'Ems. Twitch is actively looking for partners to build extension inventory — BLAST was approached for Austin but couldn't afford external dev costs. We'd be first movers.

Lulu (Product/Eng) scoped a minimal overlay extension: Steam login → check Valve viewer pass → pass picks to Valve. Estimated cost provided. Georg (Commercial Ops) sees a bigger play — a holistic EFG extension with geo-targeted commercial entitlements, data widgets, and Grid as data backbone.

## The Problem

Three different things are being discussed as one:

| Workstream | What it is | Reusable? | Revenue model |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pick'Ems overlay** (Lulu's scope) | Valve Major-specific, Steam login + picks passthrough | No — Major-only throwaway code | Chexx (at risk — licensing) |
| **Holistic EFG extension** (Georg's vision) | Geo-targeted overlays, promo codes, drops, data widgets | Yes — that's the point | Brand partnerships, multiple verticals |
| **Twitch relationship play** (Fahad's strategy) | Use Pick'Ems as trojan horse to unlock future commercialisation | N/A | Strategic investment |

## Open Questions (from Lulu — unanswered)

1. **What is the aim from a brand partnership POV?** Drive conversions (account creation) or brand awareness (skinning only)?
2. **Overlay extension or something else?**
3. **Acknowledgement that this is Valve-specific one-off code?**

## Key Risks

- **Chexx licensing:** Georg flagged Chexx is "at risk to be blocked by Twitch for lack of a license." If Chexx is the commercial layer for Pick'Ems, this undermines the entire revenue case.
- **No committed revenue:** Nobody in the thread named a number. Dev cost is real; revenue is hypothetical.
- **Scope creep:** The "holistic extension" is being used to retroactively justify a one-off cost. These are different projects with different timelines.
- **Timeline:** IEM Cologne Major has a fixed date. Delayed decisions = wasted discovery.
- **Historical blocker:** 12 months ago Twitch didn't allow promoting anything besides FACEIT on extensions. Fahad says they're now open "case by case" — need written confirmation.

## My Assessment

The strategic opportunity is real. Being first to commercialise Twitch extensions for esports is high-ceiling. But the current conversation is conflating a throwaway Major integration with a platform play.

### Recommendation

**Separate the workstreams:**

**Short-term: Pick'Ems Extension (decide by end of next week)**
- Only build if: (a) a partner commits revenue covering dev cost, OR (b) leadership explicitly approves as strategic Twitch relationship investment with no immediate ROI
- Fahad must answer Lulu's 3 questions before any further scoping
- Clarify Chexx licensing risk — if blocked, what's the fallback commercial layer?

**Medium-term: Holistic EFG Extension (separate kickoff)**
- Genuine opportunity — geo-targeted entitlements, Grid data backbone, multi-partner inventory
- Needs its own scoping with Product + Commercial Ops + Grid
- Should NOT be gated on the Pick'Ems decision
- This is where the real NSM value lives (new inventory type for brand partnerships)
