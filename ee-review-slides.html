Coverage Gaps

EE doesn't cover the highest-value challenge types

Trade (Tradeit) and Buy (WhiteMarket) are 100% click-dependent but have zero EE coverage. These are the highest-value partner actions.

Challenge Click-Dependency

Challenge Clicked CR No Notif CR Click Dependency
Trade (Tradeit) 11.95% 1.22% 9.8× dependent
Buy (WhiteMarket) 2.85% 0.17% 16.8× dependent
Deposit (Winline) 16.55% 8.71% 1.9× dependent
Account Link 8.93% 4.11% 2.2× dependent
Play Match 97.51% 84.20% 1.2× (baseline)

Click Dependency = Clicked CR / No Notification CR

Bet and Win Match: suppress — Negative completion uplift across all partners. Bet: −3.47pp, Win Match: variable. Sending these notifications costs attention without converting.

EE expansion opportunity — If EE's 2.6× completion advantage applies to Trade and Buy, we'd see ~31% Trade CR (from 11.95%) and ~7.4% Buy CR (from 2.85%). These are the partner actions that directly drive NSM.

Proposal

Proposed actions — for discussion

  • 1
    Prioritise Reward Motivation as default framework
    12.19% full CR is 2× the next best. Make it the primary framework for all partners and campaigns.
  • 2
    Expand EE to Trade + Buy challenges
    These are the highest click-dependency actions (9.8× and 16.8×). EE's 2.6× advantage would directly multiply partner value.
  • 3
    Suppress Bet and Win Match notifications
    Negative completion uplift across all partners. Zero conversion cost, reduces notification fatigue.
  • 4
    Test frequency capping
    Sawtooth decay suggests users fatigue within 2-3 weeks. A/B test 3-day vs 7-day frequency cap to see if we can sustain higher CTR.
  • 5
    Investigate campaign-launch timing
    CTR spikes 25-35% at launch. Can we stagger framework rotation to maintain novelty?

Discussion points: Do we agree on framework prioritisation? What's the implementation timeline for EE on Trade/Buy? Any concerns about suppressing Bet/Win?

Related Investigation

Activity Notifications — separate workstream

The EE review answers 'are our psychological frameworks working?' A separate question: is the overall notification setup (what, when, who) optimal?

What we know

  • Full cohort analysis complete (Clicked vs Impression vs No Notification)
  • Per-partner breakdown available (Winline, Tradeit, WhiteMarket)
  • Premium missions: 90-97% completion without notifications — low ROI
  • Sponsored missions: near-zero baseline without notifications — highest ROI

Open questions

  • Should we experiment with notification timing (time of day, day of week)?
  • Is the current notification mix optimal, or are we over-notifying?
  • Can we personalise notification framework per user behaviour?
