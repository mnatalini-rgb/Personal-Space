1. Experiment Design

Background

Problem: Interstitial ads deliver our highest CPMs due to strong visibility and CTR, but they are highly intrusive. Increasing interstitial volume risks user frustration, reduced session depth, and long-term CPM degradation. We cannot safely grow revenue by increasing frequency.

Solution: Maintain the same inventory size but increase CPMs by upgrading the creative format — replace static image interstitials with video. Video drives stronger advertiser outcomes (viewability, CTR, brand lift) at the same impression count.

Why skippable: Given the format’s intrusiveness, skippable video (3s unskippable, then skip available) balances revenue uplift with user experience. This aligns with established user expectations (YouTube-like behaviour) and preserves advertiser impact in the opening seconds.

Key Design Choice

No Paid Cannibalisation

The video interstitial only fires when no display interstitial fills from the programmatic waterfall. This means every video impression is incremental — it monetises previously unfilled inventory rather than displacing higher-CPM paid ads.

Guardrail Passed

Variant tracks within ±1K of control on all 31 days. The +0.3% aggregate delta is not statistically significant — both groups show identical weekly seasonality (Sat/Sun peaks at ~190–197K, midweek troughs at ~151–165K). No retention divergence detected at any point in the experiment window.

Weekly Pattern

Both groups exhibit a strong 7-day cycle: matchmaking uniques peak on weekends (Sat 190K+) and trough midweek (Tue/Wed ~151–165K). The video interstitial does not dampen or shift this natural engagement rhythm.

7. Decision Framework

Key Questions

Does the video hurt retention?

No. Variant (+0.3% daily active matchmaking players) is flat to marginally positive vs control. Guardrail passed.

Do users watch the full video?

83.3% of FE interactions are "completed" (full 15s). GAM weighted completion rate is 70.4% across 184 countries. Strong engagement signal.

Do users click the CTA?

0.37% CTR across 6.47M impressions = 23,698 clicks to the Premium purchase page. These clicks drove 320 Premium subscriptions (46 direct, 274 assisted via view-through), a 1.35% click-to-subscription conversion rate.

Is completion rate consistent globally?

71 countries achieve ≥70% completion. Central Asia / Balkans dip to 50–65% (likely device/connectivity). APAC and Americas consistently 75–81%.

Next Steps

  1. Guardrail passed — no retention regression detected. Safe to proceed.
  2. Validate CPM uplift: compare video eCPM vs display interstitial eCPM for the same unfilled inventory.
  3. Explore replacing house ad with programmatic demand (VAST tags from SSPs) — revenue opportunity.
  4. Monitor low-completion geos (Kosovo, Pakistan, Albania) — consider geo-targeting adjustments if video CPM < display CPM in those markets.

Go-to-Market — Open Questions for Commercial Team

The experiment validated the format on a new ad unit using a house ad (Premium subscription CTA). Mandar’s team has agreed to take skippable video interstitials to market. The following questions need answers from the commercial side to define the operational model:

1. Ad Serving Setup

The experiment ran as a custom front-end implementation (house ad, no GAM video line item). How should this work in production? Options include VAST tags from SSPs, direct-sold creatives via GAM, or a hybrid approach.

2. Sales & Packaging

How will the sales team position this to advertisers? As a standalone product, bundled with display interstitials, or as part of a broader video package? What’s the pitch?

3. Pricing Model

What pricing structure works for the market? CPM floor, fixed rate per campaign, or programmatic-only? The experiment data (70.4% completion, 0.37% CTR) provides the proof points — sales needs to translate this into a rate card.

4. Trafficking & Operations

Who handles creative upload, QA, and campaign setup? Does this fit into the existing ad ops workflow or does it require new tooling / process?

Owner: Mandar’s commercial team. PM has validated the format works and there is no retention risk. The operational go-to-market model is a commercial decision.
